A Little More About Me

I grew up in a family of five, being the youngest of my siblings. My older brother Lawrence has always been faster, stronger, and smarter than I am. He's currently at seminary to become a catholic priest. My oldest sibling is my sister Caroline. She has always been more book-smart, but can't think as fast under pressure. The three of us used to love spending time together outside from swimming in the hot summers to sledding in the cold winters.

Growing up, I tried and practicied many different hobbies and sports to find my interests and my talents. I played basketball, golf, baseball, swimming, gymnastics, but I certianly never excelled in any of these. They were fun but everyone I played with was always better. When I tried ice skating, I never could stand away from the wall without falling down. It wasn't until I played video games til I found my specialties. I was a natural at my age, with hand-eye coordination and quick reaction time, I finally found something I could win at against my friends and family. I think that's the beginning factor into why I love computer science now.

Now that I'm at university, I've started learning to develop code to run my own projects. I've enjoyed learning to use a variety of coding languages for various tasks, each with their own unique structure and formats. Coding is hard, and it takes me longer than I expected to become specialized with; to me, coding is like a game: developing pieces and structures to claim that victory, to create something new, something useful.
